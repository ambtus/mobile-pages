Chapter 5: Weeping Light, Tested Coward

Chapter Text

Iruka stared numbly at the bowl of ramen. He'd only just tried to make amends with Naruto, and now the kid was gone.

And with a pile of rumors running after him.

The most predominant one was that 'the Kyuubi kid' had helped Itachi kill the Uchihas, and then left with him, closely followed by ‘Itachi controlled the Kyuubi’.

(Someone suggested that Itachi had taken Sasuke and Naruto when he left.)

But Naruto was not the Kyuubi, Iruka knew that now.

So no, Iruka did not trust this whole debacle at all, especially because of Sasuke's involvement: the young Uchiha had always loved his brother, and whenever Itachi had picked up the boy from the Academy there had been only love there.

Iruka believed exactly one thing from all the rumors: Sasuke’s body was not found.

Because Iruka did not believe that Itachi would kill his brother.

That left the question of where Naruto had gone. There was no reported struggle (not that anyone would have paid attention) and nothing was seen or heard since.

Which was exactly why Iruka knew that Naruto was still alive somewhere. If Naruto was dead, then the Kyuubi would be free.

~~~

Naruto crouched on top of the cliff he’d run to after Shi-nii fell asleep, looking out at the ocean.

He was a Jinchuuriki.

He was an Uzumaki.

He was a Namikaze.

He was a Clan Heir.

He was the son of a Hokage.

He was…

He was crying.

“Uzushio?” Naruto wiped tears from his face. “Do you… know what Mama looked like, dattebayo?” He knew what D- no, that still felt strange- what the Yondaime looked like, but not Kushina.

“ I know what all of My people look like, little Fox, ” Uzushio hummed on the wind. “ Her hair was not unlike my Human form’s, her eyes violet, and her strength and love nigh-unmatched. ”

“But-” Naruto sniffled, looking down. “Why did she put the Kyuubi into me, ‘ttebayo? Didn’t she know they would hate me?”

“ She hated that she had to, little Fox, ” Uzushio soothed. “ She hated that she didn’t have another choice, and she hated that she wouldn’t be able to keep you safe. ”

“But why DIDN’T she have another choice?!” Naruto demanded, shooting to his feet.

“ The Bijuu are not mindless monsters, little Fox, ” Uzushio sighed sadly. “ Kurama knew that you were poised to be his next container. Were he not sealed into you, he would have killed you to keep himself free. ”

“Oh…”

“What did you call her?”

Uzushio laughed. “ She was My little Firecracker, despite her lack of a Fire nature. ”

“Why wasn’t she little Fox too?” Naruto ran his fingers through the red dirt. “She was a Jinchuuriki, dattebayo.”

“ Oh little Fox, ” Uzushio crooned. “ She was a Jinchuuriki, yes, but not like you are. You lived alongside Kurama since the very beginning. Why do you think you are able to change your form to claws and fangs without Kurama’s assistance? ”

“Kurama?” Naruto blinked, finally registering the name. “Are you calling the Kyuubi ‘Kurama’?”

“ The names of the Bijuu are indeed not well known, ” Uzushio hummed.

“Oh…” Naruto made a pile of dirt, poking holes in it with his claws. “Why do you know them?”

“ A few of them found my existence curious! ” Uzushio laughed. “ Before Konoha was founded, I met some of them. ”

“Hm…” Naruto continued fiddling with the dirt.

~~~

Kakashi coughed, spitting water out of his mouth.

“Goodbye for now, little Wolf.”

Kakashi looked back up at Uzushio, who was standing on the water.

Uzushio smiled, dropping into the water and disappearing.

Kakashi stared for a moment longer before climbing onto the surface of the water and making for the half-distant shore.

~~~

Sasuke followed the tiny trail up toward the clifftops. He’d woken where he’d fallen asleep, curled into Shi-nii’s side, who was also asleep.

And Naruto was gone.

So Sasuke asked Uzushio.

Sasuke pushed through the bushes, catching sight of bright blonde on the top of the cliff. “Thanks Uzushio.”

“ Little Light. ”

Sasuke ran up, pausing a few steps behind Naruto. “Naru?”

Naruto glanced back, dry tear tracks on his face. “…Hi Suke.”

Sasuke plopped down next to Naruto, tilting his head at the small, clumsy recreation of the Hokage monument made of dirt. “Cool.”

“Hm…” Naruto continued fiddling with the dirt.

“What’re you doing up here?”

Naruto shrugged.

Sasuke was extra suspicious now. Naruto was almost never quiet, and ever since the second night in the beach cave had even been humming in his sleep. He leaned over, pressing his shoulder into Naruto’s a bit awkwardly. “It’s a nice view.”

Naruto nodded, clawing down and slowly rebuilding the monument.

-

Naruto slowly pointed at where the Fourth Hokage’s face was pressed into vague form. “That’s my dad.”

Sasuke blinked, unsure how to react. “Oh.” Hm. “He wasn’t an Uzumaki though?”

Naruto smiled sadly. “No, ‘ttebayo. That was my mom, dattebayo: Kushina.”

Sasuke perked up. “I know that name.”

Naruto whirled. “WHAT!?!”

Sasuke nodded. “She was a friend of-” deadblooddeadonthefloor “-she was one of my-” deaddeaddead “-my mom’s Genin teammates…”

“Why are you crying??!” Naruto stared at Sasuke, eyes wide. “Suke!!”

“She’s dead ,” Sasuke choked out, burying his face in his hands.

“Yeah, I-”

“Not Kushi - well yes- my mom …”

“Oh…”

~~~

Kakashi turned east one last time. I’ll be back, Naruto, Uzushio.

Kakashi ran back toward Konoha.

Kakashi ran.
