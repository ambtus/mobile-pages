Yer a Wizard, Drizzt

Merfilly

Summary:

In an AU where Zak did not persuade Malice, Drizzt acquires a protector of sorts for his odd nature.

Notes:

(See the end of the work for notes.)

Work Text:

Malice glared at the impertinent male, then moved a hand toward her whip. "His life was spared at the cost of a trained wizard, so wizard he shall be." Her eyes flicked over Dinin before coming back to Zaknafein. "Understand that I will tolerate no deviation here!" The Weapon Master nodded once, angrily.

He didn't like it, but he knew when he could push her, and this was not that time.


"Why not both?"

Rizzen shook his head. "You learn enough blade work to stay alive, but then the magic is your focus. Can't cast and slice."

He still found the boy exasperating and strange. Especially when Drizzt asked questions. And got that look, the Zaknafein look, on his features. It was one more reminder who the sire had to be, even if his features followed Malice's delicacy more.

Rizzen privately wondered if Malice should have given in on the fighter idea, just to have a new Weapon Master in the making, as much as Zaknafein pushed her and the daughters of the House with his ways.

Then again, Drizzt looked like he was cut from the exact same cloth.


Drizzt found time to practice the basic sword and dagger work he'd been taught. It could be better, and he knew it, but he had so little time to himself. He'd get better, someday.

Fortunately, Rizzen found it easier to give him tasks and material to study than spend all his time with Drizzt. So those stolen moments of practice continued, right through time to go to Sorcere.


Rarely, Gromph had reason to review the incidents of violence within Sorcere. The death of a Master certainly invoked that need. He went to interview them, and found Masoj Hun'ett was taking no chances, having silenced the young student.

Masoj stabbed a finger at Drizzt. "He killed my brother." Drizzt's lavender eyes snapped with anger over the accusation.

"Then your brother was inept," Gromph said dismissively. He studied the fifth year student. "Still, you can duel him for honor? Surely you can overcome a lowly student and take Gelroos' spot."

Gromph twisted a gesture and Drizzt found his words returned. The young one didn't say a word though, pinning Masoj back by look alone.

This was going to be interesting, Gromph decided, as Masoj's arrogance -- fear of what Drizzt knew -- made him agree.

What happened next reminded Gromph, again, that some surface ways were better, for this student could fight as well as he handled his low-level spells. He came and inspected the corpse, removing various items, and eyed the figure of wondrous power with distaste. He knew, already, that the creature it summoned was barely restrained by the figure, its nature antithetical to all things drow.

"You can keep the figure," Gromph decided, though he would not provide the name. Masoj had been Drizzt's direct teacher, so either he knew it, or he'd learn his divination spells faster to find out. "With both Hun'etts dead — "

"Pardon, Archmage, but the Faceless One screamed that his name was not Hun'ett, and said a name I did not know."

Gromph's eyebrows rose, and he realized he would have to go inspect that corpse too, before it was disposed of in the acid lake.

"You may speak the name."

"DeVir. Alton DeVir."

How very interesting.

"Do not say it again, for the House does not exist, has not for the length of your life." Gromph studied him with vulpine curiosity and calculation. "I will assign you a new teacher. Learn quickly, Do'Urden."

Drizzt inclined his head to that, and the Archmage left him.


The girl, all of maybe ten years, looked at Drizzt Do'Urden with disdain and arrogance befitting her station.

He ignored it.

"Liriel Baenre, I am Drizzt Do'Urden as I am certain the Archmage told you."

"He also said you were strange, and I was only to listen to your magic teaching."

Drizzt inclined his head to that. "I am but a student myself, and will share what I have already learned. If it pleases you, you can help me choose which schools of magic to focus on."

That got her attention, her amber eyes — as unusual as his lavender ones — meeting his fully for the first time.

"Accepted."


Gromph was pleased. He had a teacher for his hidden daughter that owed him deeply, given that Gromph continued to shield him from scrutiny of his oddities. The Do'Urden male would impart a solid magic schooling, given that he excelled in his spellwork, and likely teach her to defend herself, given his love of fighting techniques.

This would be profitable to his own goals, and if necessary, the tenth year student could be quietly disposed of before time to go to Arach-Tinilith to complete his learning.

That was years away, yet, and Gromph had plenty of time to decide how to handle it.


"My father is not going to let you survive teaching me," Liriel tapped out on Drizzt's arm as they studied a spell together.

"I know," he answered the same way.

"Why are you like you are?"

"I do not know."

Five more years, at most, and Drizzt would be eligible to graduate as a wizard, return to his home and … what?

Participate in a society that sickened and repulsed him? He'd seen too many things, read too many books that he might not have had access to except for this need of Gromph's to have the girl child schooled in magic.

Liriel did not press; she actually had warmed to him, even as she remained frosty to all others she had to interact with. If she did not know his plans, they could not take it from her mind, yet she had felt it needed to warn him.


Escaping the school had taken every bit of his skill in stealth and magic. Drizzt threaded a narrow course to one of the forbidden ruins, certain of his research.

One more year. Maybe Gromph would have done something other than kill him, given how long he'd stretched out this task. But Liriel had not come for lessons three nights in a row. Something had changed… and Drizzt was ready.

Carrying his sword, dagger, the figure, and supplies in addition to his personal spellbook, Drizzt made it past the wards and traps on the ruins, and worked his way to the chapel within.

By the records he had found, this family had been brought down for having wealth gained through trade with the surface. No one had ever found the portal they had used, but Drizzt had found one of their wizard's writings, searching for new spells to share with his student.

He knew where it was, and how to activate it.


Drizzt blinked at the light all around him, finding himself in a wild place full of things he knew were called 'trees', with the portal fading back out behind him.

He knew some words, could read more than he spoke, as they did not have pronunciation guides except to the magical words, and he knew everything he carried would disintegrate slowly.

He smiled broadly, just glad to be free of that city. He did hope Liriel landed on her feet, but she was more like drow than himself.

He lived, and where there was life, there was hope.


In Menzoberranzan, an archmage smiled. He had his own plans, ones his sister was currently meddling in. That the strangely good young wizard had chosen to flee might even be useful, some day.

For now, he had to write up a magical accident for a family, with one casualty — Drizzt Do'Urden.

Notes:

I have vague ideas of continuation, but uncertain for now. Drizzt is forty-nine at the end here, which would be about eight years after he canonically reached the surface.
